Marble Experiment

Today we conducted an experiment using a marble and a ramp. The research question we were investigating is:

What is the relationship between the velocity of a marble after rolling down a slope and the height of the ramp?

We used VHS Tape boxes as different heights, and conducted the test with 5 different heights and 5 trials for each height.

This is what the setup for the experiment looked like.

Using a LabQuest and a gate laser sensor, we measured the instantaneous velocity right when the marble came out of the ramp.

After running this experiment, I found that in my data, there is a linear relationship between the square root of the height and the velocity of the marble, as shown in this graph:

This means that as you drop the object from a higher height, the velocity of the object increases exponentially.

In this energy system, at the top of the ramp, the marble has gravitational potential energy. When it starts rolling down the ramp, that gravitational potential energy gets converted into kinetic energy.
