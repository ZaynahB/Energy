What is Work?

No, not work as in a job. Work is the change in energy. Energy is a measurement of the ability of something to do work.

The standard unit used to measure energy and work done is physics the joule, which has the symbol J. Work = Force x displacement, so 1 Joule is the energy transferred when a force of 1 Newton is applied to an object and moves it through a distance of 1 meter.

We looked at this scenario during class:

A person is standing with a backpack on them, and the backpack weighs, 10kg and is 1 meter above the ground. The person walks 2 meters to the right at a constant velocity. What is the work done on the backpack? (Excuse the drawing haha)

In this scenario, there is actually no work done on the backpack. The backpack starts at rest and ends at rest, and it always stays 1 meter above the ground. Since the person is moving at a constant velocity, there is no force being applied. This means that there is no work done on the backpack in this case.

A question I have is what happens when the force is not in the same direction as the motion of the object? This formula would not give you the correct value in that scenario.

One concept that is very intriguing to me is that in practice, whenever work is done to move energy from one form to another, there is always some loss to other forms of energy such as heat and sound. For example, a lightbulb could be 5% efficient at converting electrical energy to visible light, meaning that some energy was “lost” along the way.
