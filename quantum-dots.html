What is a solar quantum dot?

This post will be a little bit different. I was doing some research into solar technology, and I came across this one thing that I found super interesting–solar quantum dots (QDs). I'm going to use this post to synthesize the information I learnt about QDs and expain how they work.

----------

What if we could power our buildings and homes using the windows on them? Turns out, we actually can.

We can do this by layering windows with tiny, invisible solar cells called quantum dots. Quantum dots are extremely small nanoparticles–one quantum dot is the size of 1/10000 of the width of a single strand of a human’s hair. Because they are so small, they operate using the quantum mechanics, hence the name quantum dots.

Currently, one application of quantum dots are being used to produce better TVs. When you put energy into a quantum dot, it produces light, which is perfect for a TV. But since quantum mechanics is linear, when you put light into a quantum dot, it produces energy, which means that we can use this technology to harness energy.

Quantum dots solar cells are different than traditional solar cells for a few different reasons.

1) Solar panels can only harness light from the visible spectrum and a little bit of infrared. Quantum dots absorb all wavelengths of light depending on the size, and they let visible light pass through. This is why we would be able use windows to harness energy. Since they let visible light pass through, we could have clear solar panels and use them as windows of buildings. A transparent coating of quantum dots could be painted on windows or they can be used as their own clear solar panel and that could be used as the window itself.

2) When you change the size of a quantum dot, it is able to absorb a different wavelength of light. When you layer different sized of QDs, you are able to absorb all of the different wavelengths. Because of this, quantum dots are 30% more efficient compared to traditional solar panels in terms of the amount of light they are able to harness.

3) The energy payback time for QD solar cells is incredibly lower than silicon solar cells. Energy payback time is how long it takes for a solar panel to produce as much energy as it took to manufacture it. Quantum dots are made through a process called colloidal deposition (a fancy name for nanoparticles self-assembling onto a surface), and this process allows them to be deposited at room temperature, meaning that their manufacturing process requires significantly less energy than typical silicon solar cells.

This technology would allow us to harness solar energy in a way that we never have been able to before with incredible efficiency.
