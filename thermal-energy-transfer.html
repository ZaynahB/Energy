Thermal Energy Transfer

Today we looked into how thermal energy is transferred. We created mind maps with the key word “heat”. Here is the mind map that I created:

We looked at the 3 ways that heat is transferred: conduction, radiation, and convection.

Conduction is the heat transfer by particle collisions in a solid. For example, touching a hot stove is an example of conduction.

Radiation is a method of heat transfer that doesn’t require direct contact. Electromagnetic waves travel through space, and when these heat waves hit matter, they may be reflected, transmitted or absorbed. If they are absorbed, the particles in the matter may increase their movement and the overall temperature increases. An example of radiation is heat from the sun warming your face.

Convection is heat transfer in liquids and gases by a circulating path of fluid. Convection occurs when warmer areas of a liquid or gas rise to cooler areas in the liquid or gas. As this happens, cooler liquid or gas takes the place of the warmer areas. This cycle results in a continuous circulation pattern and heat is transferred to cooler areas. One example of this is when you boil water in a pot on the stove. The bubbles of water that rise are the hotter parts of the water rising to the cooler area of the water at the top of the pan.

These are the 3 ways that thermal energy is transferred: conduction, radiation, and convection.
