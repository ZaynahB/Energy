The Types of Energy

This class, we learnt about the different types of energy. Energy is something that we can’t actually see or view, but we still know that it is there. There are different ways that energy can be stored, and these are called storage mechanisms. However, the energy itself doesn’t change, it is just stored in different ways.

Storage Mechanisms

Kinetic (k) – stored in motion of object
Thermal (th) – stored in motion of microscopic particles in object
Gravitational Potential (g) – stored in interaction between earth and object (Earth’s gravitational field)
Elastic (e) – stored in stretch of object (change in shape)
Chemical (c) – stored in bonds (interactions) between particles

For example, the pool stick in motion has kinetic energy. This kinetic energy gets transferred into the ball that it hits, which then gets transferred into the second ball, also as kinetic energy.

Using a diagram, we can look at the way energy is stored at two moments in time. This is called an LOL diagram. It has two separate bar graphs which show the amount of energy stored in each mechanism at two different moments in time. In between the two bar graphs, there is a circle for the energy flow diagram.

Another concept we talked about is Work. The energy of a system can change due to a force acting on it during a displacement. The energy change of the system due to a force is called work. The amount of work when the force points in the same direction as the object’s displacement is positive and given by W = F∆d.

A question I have still is what exactly is energy? Yes we know that it exists, but what actually is it? This is something that I am not too clear on yet. Also, I wonder who discovered all of these different types of energy and what was the process they used for going about this? Because energy is something we can’t see, it would take very unique thinking to conceptualize something like it.

One thing I found interesting is the concept of black holes. It is kind of like energy, you can’t see it, but we still know that it is there because of the great force of attraction. The fact that the gravitational acceleration of a black hole is so strong that nothing–even light–can escape it is insane to even think about. In the center of black hole, a one dimensional point called a gravitational singularity exists, which contains a huge mass in an indefinitely small space where density and gravity become infinite. At this point, the laws of physics as we know it cease to operate. This is such a crazy concept to me, that there is a point so dense that nothing works “normally”.
